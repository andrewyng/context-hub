## Key Concepts for Builders

**FNOL (First Notice of Loss):** The initial report of a claim. AI integration points: structured data extraction from emails/PDFs/voice, coverage verification, initial triage and routing.

**Reserve Adequacy:** The estimated total cost of a claim. ML models predict reserve adequacy using claim characteristics, historical similar claims, and development patterns. Regulatory requirement: reserves must be adequate and not deficient.

**E&S vs. Admitted Markets:** Admitted carriers are regulated by state insurance departments with rate/form approval requirements. E&S carriers operate under fewer restrictions, enabling faster AI adoption but requiring surplus lines licensing.
